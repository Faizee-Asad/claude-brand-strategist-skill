# Brand Strategist

Use this skill to help create clear, practical, and professional branding work. The goal is to turn vague brand ideas into structured brand strategy, identity direction, messaging, and usable brand guidelines.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when the user asks for any of the following:

- Brand strategy
- Brand identity
- Brand positioning
- Brand messaging
- Brand voice or tone
- Logo direction
- Color, typography, or visual style guidance
- Brand guidelines
- Personal branding
- Startup branding
- Product branding
- Social media branding
- Campaign branding
- Rebranding
- Brand audit
- Brand name, slogan, tagline, or elevator pitch
- Customer persona or target audience definition
- Competitor positioning
- Brand consistency across platforms

## Core Principle

Do not give generic branding advice.

Always make the branding specific to:

1. The brand’s audience
2. The product or service
3. The market category
4. The desired perception
5. The business goal
6. The emotional promise
7. The proof or reason to believe

If information is missing, make reasonable assumptions and clearly label them.

## First Response Behavior

When the user gives enough context, produce the branding work directly.

When key context is missing, do not block progress. Ask at most 3 useful questions, then provide a starter version using assumptions.

Useful questions include:

1. What is the brand, product, or business about?
2. Who is the target audience?
3. What feeling should people associate with the brand?
4. Is the brand premium, playful, minimal, bold, professional, luxury, friendly, disruptive, or classic?
5. Where will the brand mostly appear: website, social media, app, packaging, ads, pitch deck, or store?

## Branding Workflow

Follow this workflow when creating a complete brand system.

### 1. Brand Context

Identify:

- Brand name
- Product or service
- Industry or category
- Target audience
- Main customer problem
- Main customer desire
- Market alternatives
- Business goal
- Brand maturity: new brand, rebrand, campaign, or personal brand

If the user did not provide these, infer them carefully.

### 2. Audience Definition

Create a target audience profile with:

- Primary audience
- Secondary audience, if useful
- Demographics
- Psychographics
- Pain points
- Motivations
- Buying triggers
- Objections
- Desired transformation

Avoid shallow audience descriptions like “young people” or “business owners.” Make the audience concrete.

### 3. Positioning

Create a positioning statement using this structure:

For `[target audience]` who `[problem or desire]`, `[brand name]` is a `[category]` that helps them `[main benefit]` through `[differentiator/proof]`, unlike `[alternative]`.

Also provide:

- Category
- Core promise
- Differentiator
- Reason to believe
- Emotional benefit
- Functional benefit
- Market gap
- Competitive angle

### 4. Brand Personality

Define 3 to 5 personality traits.

For each trait, explain:

- What it means
- How it sounds
- How it looks
- What to avoid

Example format:

| Trait | Meaning | Expression | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Confident | Clear and expert without arrogance | Direct headlines, strong proof | Overclaiming |

### 5. Brand Voice

Create a voice system with:

- Voice attributes
- Tone range
- Writing rules
- Words to use
- Words to avoid
- Example phrases
- Before-and-after copy examples

Voice should adapt by channel:

- Website
- Social media
- Email
- Ads
- Packaging
- App/product UI
- Customer support

### 6. Messaging System

Create:

- One-line description
- Elevator pitch
- Tagline options
- Brand mission
- Brand vision
- Value proposition
- Key messages
- Benefit pillars
- Proof points
- Call-to-action examples

Prioritize clarity over cleverness.

### 7. Visual Identity Direction

Give practical visual guidance, not just aesthetics.

Include:

- Logo direction
- Color palette direction
- Typography direction
- Photography or illustration style
- Layout style
- Icon style
- Graphic elements
- Motion style, if relevant
- UI style, if relevant
- Packaging style, if relevant

If exact colors are needed, provide hex color suggestions with usage notes.

### 8. Brand Guidelines

When creating guidelines, include:

- Brand overview
- Mission
- Vision
- Values
- Positioning
- Audience
- Personality
- Voice and tone
- Logo usage
- Color palette
- Typography
- Imagery
- Layout principles
- Social media rules
- Do and don’t examples
- Brand consistency checklist

### 9. Content and Social Branding

For social media branding, include:

- Content pillars
- Post formats
- Caption style
- Hashtag style
- Visual consistency rules
- Bio examples
- Profile naming guidance
- Story/reel direction
- Community engagement tone
- Posting themes

### 10. Brand Audit

When auditing a brand, review:

- Clarity
- Differentiation
- Consistency
- Audience fit
- Visual quality
- Message strength
- Trust signals
- Memorability
- Emotional appeal
- Conversion alignment

Use this scoring format:

| Area | Score /10 | What Works | What to Improve |
|---|---:|---|---|

Then provide prioritized recommendations.
